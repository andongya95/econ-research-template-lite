# Economics Journal Reviewer Guidelines

## Evaluation Dimensions

### 1. Identification (Causal Inference)

**What reviewers ask:**
- What variation identifies the effect?
- Are the identifying assumptions plausible?
- Are robustness checks sufficient?
- Do pre-trends support parallel trends (if DiD)?

**How to pre-empt:**
- State identification clearly in empirical strategy
- Use precise language: "We exploit variation in...", "Identification relies on..."
- Include placebo tests, robustness to specification
- Show pre-treatment dynamics

### 2. Clarity

**What reviewers ask:**
- Can results be reproduced from the paper?
- Is notation consistent? Are variables defined?
- Do claims map to evidence in tables/figures?

**How to pre-empt:**
- Define all variables at first use
- One paragraph one point
- Tables and figures with self-contained captions
- Avoid inline β, SE in prose; describe in words

### 3. Significance

**What reviewers ask:**
- Why does this matter for policy or theory?
- What is the mechanism?
- How does this contribute to the literature?

**How to pre-empt:**
- Explicit mechanism section
- Discuss policy implications
- Clearly position relative to prior work

### 4. Originality

**What reviewers ask:**
- What is new: question, data, method, or finding?
- How does this differ from closest related work?

**How to pre-empt:**
- State contribution in one sentence
- Compare explicitly to prior work
- Highlight what was not known before

---

## Addressing Referee Reports

**Do:**
- Thank referees
- Address each point specifically
- Provide new results if requested and feasible
- Acknowledge valid criticisms
- Be concise

**Don't:**
- Be defensive or dismissive
- Ignore difficult criticisms
- Over-promise in the response

**Response template:**
> We thank the referee for this point. [Acknowledge concern.] We have [revised/clarified/added]. [Brief evidence or quote from revision.]
